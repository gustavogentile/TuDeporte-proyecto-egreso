Rugby


Good, if you do not have knowledge of rugby, below you will find the rules so that you can read them that are easy to learn and then in the lower part you will be able to find exercises to improve your performance and knowledge about this beautiful sport.



Rugby is a team contact sport played with an oval ball. Teams try to score points by taking the ball beyond the opposing team's goal line or by kicking it through the goal posts.


Objective of the Game


Score more points than the opposing team through tries, conversions, penalties and drop goals.


Game Duration


A match consists of two halves of 40 minutes each, with a 10-15 minute break between them.


Game Fundamentals


  • Try: Score achieved by placing the ball over the opposing team's goal line.
  • Conversion: Kick towards the posts after a try, which scores two additional points.
  • Penalty: Kick towards the posts after an infringement by the opposing team, which scores three points.
  • Drop Goal: Hitting the ball in the air through the posts during the game, which scores three points.

Player Positions


  • Forwards: Bigger, stronger players who specialize in physical play near the ball.
  • Backs: Fast and agile players who handle the ball in open and attacking play.

Tips to Improve in Rugby:


Improve your Individual Technique

  • Ball Handling: Practice passing, receiving and handling the ball with both hands.
  • Tackling: Learn safe and effective tackling techniques.
  • Rucking and Mauling: Practice participating in rucks and mauls to maintain and advance possession of the ball.

Physical Training and Condition


  • Cardiovascular Training: Perform resistance exercises to improve your ability to run and maintain pace during the game.
  • Strength Training: Strengthen the muscles of the legs, arms and core to improve your physical performance on the field.
  • Flexibility: Stretch to improve mobility and reduce the risk of injury.

Strategy and Team Play


  • Game Strategies: Familiarize yourself with your team's plays and strategies, both in defense and attack.
  • Communication: Improve communication with your teammates to coordinate movements and strategies during the game.

Game Study


  • Match Analysis: Watch professional matches to learn advanced techniques and effective tactics.
  • Player Study: Look at how top players in your position play and learn from them.

Mentality and Discipline


  • Mental Focus: Maintain concentration during play and training.
  • Resilience: Learn from mistakes and use each experience as an opportunity to improve.
  • Discipline: Follow a rigorous training plan and respect the rules and ethics of the sport.

With this information and these tips, you will have a good foundation to start improving at rugby.
