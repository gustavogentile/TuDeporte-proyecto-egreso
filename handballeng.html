Handball


Good, if you do not have knowledge of handball, below you will find the rules so that you can read them that are easy to learn and then in the lower part you will be able to find exercises to improve your performance and knowledge about this beautiful sport.



Handball is a team sport in which two teams of seven players each (six field players plus a goalkeeper) face each other to score goals by throwing a ball with their hands towards the opposing team's goal.


Objective of the Game


Score more goals than the opposing team by throwing the ball into the goal.


Game Duration


A match is played in two halves of 30 minutes each, with a break of 10-15 minutes between them.


Score


  • A goal is scored when the ball completely crosses the goal line.
  • Players can move with the ball by taking three steps or keeping it bouncing every three seconds.

Game Fundamentals


  • Pass: Transfer the ball between teammates.
  • Reception: Catching and controlling the ball after a pass.
  • Shot: Throw the ball towards the opposing team's goal to score a goal.
  • Defense: Prevent the opposing team from scoring by blocking passes and shots.

Player Positions


  • Goalkeeper: The only player who can touch the ball with any part of the body within the goal area.
  • Wingers: Players who play on the sidelines of the field and generally have quick and shooting skills.
  • Pivots: Players who play close to the goal and specialize in shots and physical contact plays.
  • Centers: Versatile players who work in both defense and attack, controlling the flow of the game.

Tips to Improve in Handball:


Improve your Individual Technique


  • Passing and Receiving: Spend time perfecting the accuracy and speed of your passes and receptions.
  • Shooting: Practice different types of shots (from different angles and distances) to improve your scoring ability.
  • Defense: Work on blocking and marking techniques to make it difficult for the opposing team to shoot.

Physical Training and Condition


  • Cardiovascular Training: Perform resistance exercises to improve your ability to run and maintain pace throughout the game.
  • Strength Training: Strengthen the muscles of your legs, arms and core to improve your performance on the field.
  • Flexibility: Stretch to improve mobility and reduce the risk of injury.

Strategy and Team Play


  • Tactical Moves: Practice tactical plays and team strategies to take advantage of the opposing team's weaknesses.
  • Communication: Improve communication with your teammates to coordinate movements and strategies during the game.

Game Study


  • Match Analysis: Watch professional matches to learn advanced techniques and effective tactics.
  • Player Study: Look at how top players in your position play and learn from them.

Mentality and Discipline


  • Mental Focus: Maintain concentration during play and training.
  • Resilience: Learn from mistakes and use each experience as an opportunity to improve.
  • Discipline: Follow a rigorous training plan and respect the rules and ethics of the sport.

With this information and these tips, you will have a good foundation to start improving at handball.
