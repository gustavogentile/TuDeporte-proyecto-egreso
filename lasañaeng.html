Chicken Lasagna with Peppers

“When you have leftover chicken in the fridge, there is nothing better than reserving it to make a good lasagna. We bring you a simple and at the same time exquisite recipe with which to dispose of those leftover chicken. Enjoy it!”

Ingredients

Difficulty: Low

Time: 40'

Servings: 2

Fresh ingredients

  • Roasted chicken: 240 grams
  • Onion: 1/2 unit
  • Red pepper: 1 unit
  • Green pepper: 1 unit
  • Grated cheese: 2 handfuls

    • Pantry ingredients

    • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
    • Bechamel sauce: 3/4 small jar
    • Lasagna sheet: 4 units
    • Fried tomato: 3 tablespoons

    Steps

    1. Preheat the oven to 200ºC.
    2. Cut the onion into thin slices and the red and green pepper into small pieces.
    3. Pour oil into a frying pan and sauté the vegetables you just chopped in it over medium heat. Cook them for about 10 minutes, until they soften.
    4. Add the roasted and shredded chicken to the pan, and mix with the vegetables.
    5. In a bowl, put a couple of tablespoons of bechamel sauce and one of fried tomato and stir. Pour the mixture over the chicken with vegetables, this way you will make the filling more juicy.
    6. Place a layer of bechamel sauce (you can mix it with a little tomato sauce). Place a couple of lasagna sheets on top. Fill with half the chicken with vegetables. Place two sheets of lasagna again. Spread a little bechamel sauce on top and finish filling with the rest of the chicken. Close with the last two sheets.
    7. Spread the rest of the bechamel sauce and optionally a little more tomato sauce on top.
    8. Put a little grated cheese on top and put it in the oven. Cook for about 15-20 minutes in the oven. The time will depend on what the pasta manufacturer's instructions indicate.
