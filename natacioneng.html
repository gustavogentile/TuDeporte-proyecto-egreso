Swimming


Good, if you do not have knowledge of swimming, below you will find the rules so that you can read them that are easy to learn and then in the lower part you will be able to find exercises to improve your performance and knowledge about this beautiful sport.



Swimming is an aquatic sport in which swimmers use various swimming styles to move through the water. It is both a recreational and competitive activity.


Swimming Styles


  • Crol (Freestyle): Most common swim in which swimmers use an alternating movement of arms and kicks.
  • Backstroke (Backstroke): I swim on the back with alternating movement of arms and kicks.
  • Breaststroke: Simultaneous movement of arms and kicks in a ventral position.
  • Butterfly: Undulating movement of the arms simultaneously above the head with undulating kicks.

Basic Rules


Objective of the Game


Complete given distances as quickly as possible using swimming techniques.


Competition


Competitions may include individual races and relays, where teams take turns swimming parts of a longer distance.


Tips to Improve in Swimming:


Improve your Swimming Technique


  • Breathing Technique: Practice breathing regularly and efficiently during each stroke.
  • Kicks: Work on the strength and coordination of your kicks to maintain fluid movement.
  • Body Position: Keep your body aligned and horizontal to reduce resistance in the water.

Endurance and Speed ​​Training


  • Distance Training: Do long distance training to improve your endurance.
  • Interval Training: Alternate between periods of fast swimming and recovery to improve speed and endurance.

Improved Strength and Flexibility


  • Secondary Training: Perform strength exercises out of the water to strengthen the muscles used in swimming, especially the core, arms and legs.
  • Stretching: Perform regular stretches to improve flexibility and reduce the risk of injury.

Competition Tactics and Strategy


  • Starts and Turns: Practice fast starts and efficient turns to improve your times in competitions.
  • Pace Control: Learn to manage your energy during races to maintain a constant pace.

Mentality and Discipline


  • Mental Focus: Maintain concentration during competitions and training.
  • Resilience: Learn from your experiences in the water and use each swim as an opportunity to improve.
  • Discipline: Follow a regular training plan and respect the rules and sportsmanship in all competitions.


With this information and these tips, you will have a good foundation to start improving in swimming.
