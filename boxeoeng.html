Boxing


Good, if you do not have knowledge of boxing, below you will find the rules so that you can read them that are easy to learn and then in the lower part you will be able to find exercises to improve your performance and knowledge about this beautiful sport.



Boxing is a combat sport in which two opponents fight each other using only their fists, with the aim of hitting the opponent while avoiding his blows.


Basic Rules


Objective of the Game


Win the fight by hitting the opponent while avoiding their blows.


Combat Duration


The fights are divided into rounds of 3 minutes each, with 1-minute breaks between rounds. The number of rounds depends on the level of the fight, generally between 4 and 12 rounds.


Score


  • Clean and effective hits to the head and body score points.
  • Bights can be won by knockout (KO), when a boxer cannot continue, or by judges' decision based on points.

Game Fundamentals


Position: Maintain good posture with legs bent and weight distributed evenly.


Basics:

  • Jab: Straight punch with the front hand.
  • Direct: Straight hit with the back hand.
  • Hook: Curved blow to the sides.
  • Upper: Upward blow towards the chin.
  • Defense: Learn to block, dodge and counterattack.
  • Movements: Use head movements and movements to control the distance and pace of combat.

Tips to Improve in Boxing:


Improve your Physical Condition


  • Cardiovascular Training: Run, jump rope, and do high-intensity exercises to improve your endurance.
  • Muscle Strengthening: Perform strength exercises to strengthen the muscles in your legs, arms and core.
  • Flexibility: Stretch to improve flexibility and reduce the risk of injury.

Practice your Techniques


  • Strikes: Spend time perfecting the technique of each basic and advanced strike.
  • Combos: Practice combinations of blows to develop fluidity and speed.
  • Defense: Work on your defense, practicing blocks, dodges and counterattacks.

Sparring and Tactical Training


  • Sparring: Train with peers of similar level to practice techniques and strategies in a controlled environment.
  • Strategy: Develop strategies to adapt to different fighting styles and rivals.

Study Sports


  • Analyze Fights: Watch professional fights to learn advanced tactics and techniques.
  • Technical Study: Learn from coaches and experts about specific tactics and training strategies.

Mentality and Discipline


  • Mental Focus: Maintain concentration during combat and training.
  • Resilience: Learn from mistakes and maintain a positive attitude to continually improve.
  • Discipline: Follow a rigorous training plan and respect the rules and ethics of the sport.

With this information and these tips, you will have a good foundation to start improving in boxing.
